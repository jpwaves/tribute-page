Makoto (Mako) Iwamatsu

An accomplished Japanese-American actor and voice-actor who touched the hearts of many.

Mako had many achievements in his life, so here is a summary of his greatest achievements, as well as a timeline of parts of Mako's life. Timeline of Mako's Life:

  • 1933: Born in Kobe, Japan on December 10, 1933.
  • 1956: Moved to the USA after becoming a naturalized American citizen, studied architecture.
  • 1966: Nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for playing Po-Han in The Sand Pebbles.
  • 1970s-1990s: Featured in many movies, some with famous actors like Jackie Chan (The Big Brawl), Arnold Schwarzengger (Conan the Barbarian and Conan the Destroyer), and Chuck Norris (An Eye for an Eye).
  • 2005: Last leading role as an actor (for the film Cages).
  • 2005: Joined the voice acting cast for Avatar: The Last Airbender as the beloved Uncle Iroh.
  • 2006: Passed away due to esophageal cancer> Before his death, he recorded the song "Leaves from the Vine" which premiered in ATLA Season 2, Episode 15, "The Tales of Ba Sing Se" when Uncle Iroh was memorializing his departed son, Lu Ten. The entire episode was dedicated to paying a tribute to Mako after his passing.
  • 2006: Received a motion picture star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.

If you are interested in learning more about Mako's incredible life, visit his Wikipedia page or his IMDb page.
